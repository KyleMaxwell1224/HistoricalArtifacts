Rabbit Hole!

Starting point: When I was a kid exploring museums, I never really thought about the formal concept of a 'Grand Staircase' in museums. Where did this concept originate from, and why?

  1. I stumbled upon this article which ranks some of the top grand staircases in museums in the world. On this list, ranked at number 1 was the Carnegie Museum of Art and Natural History, which was very interesting.
  2. I couldn't find any formal history or definition of the term Grand Staircase, but I was able to find some pretty cool examples of other Grand Staircases
    • Grand Staircase in the White House
    • The Titanic had a Grand Staircase
    • Kunsthistorisches Museum, Vienna
  3. I went to Vienna, Austria in 2016, but I never visited this museum, so I was interested in learning more about the history of this particular staircase. It was pretty difficult to find any information about the staircase on the actual website, but I did notice they do not call their staircase a Grand Staircase. They call it a stiegenhaus, whichjust translates to 'staircase'. However, their are a lot of really cool paintings on this staircase.
