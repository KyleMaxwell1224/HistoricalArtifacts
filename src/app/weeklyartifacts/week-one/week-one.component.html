Week One Reflection

9/05/21

For my reflection this week, I chose "Slow Looking". After reading the Slow Looking handout, I wanted to reflect on a still-image from Beyoncé and Jay-Z’s APESHIT video that stood out to me using Shari Tishman's Categories to Guide the Eye.

  1. What colors do you see?
    • Warm colors, especially the redish wall.
    • The white in the image really sticks out to me, both in the painting and the headpiece the women are wearing.
    • The gold around the frames also stands out.
    • The way that the woman in the painting is brightly lit stands out, almost like a 'spotlight'.
  2. What shapes or basic forms do you see?
    • The first two forms I see are the only 'alive' models, which are the two girls laying on the floor facing opposite of each other, sitting far apart but connected by a white headpiece.
    • The next form I see is directly above the two alive women, which is a painting of a woman sitting on a sofa and wearing a white dress.
    • There are paintings on either side of the painting of the woman, but they are clearly out of focus and not the prime viewing point for the viewer.
  3. What lines do you see?
    • The first line that stands out to me is the line connecting the two women on the floor, which creates a symmetrical line.
    • The borders of the paintings have very strong lines, luring the viewer to look at the art contained within the frame.
    • There appears to be security lines that are closer to the paintings, which are connected on the floor by what is at least 3 visible poles.
